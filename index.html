Naturopathy - Natural Science at Work

Naturopathy is a comprehensive, person-centred system of healthcare that emphasises prevention and the body’s inherent capacity for self-healing. Through the thoughtful use of diet, lifestyle medicine, and naturally derived therapeutics, naturopathic care aims to restore balance and support optimal health.

Naturopathic physicians combine traditional medical wisdom with modern scientific research and a deep understanding of human biology. This integrative approach allows for personalised care that considers the whole person — not just isolated symptoms.

At Como Health Group, our focus is on identifying and addressing the underlying causes of illness. Many chronic health conditions are influenced by modifiable factors such as nutrition, stress, environmental exposures, gut health, sleep, and lifestyle patterns. By investigating these drivers of disease, we aim to create sustainable improvements in health and wellbeing.

Our Clinical Approach For Naturopathy

Our clinical approach incorporates elements of functional medicine, using targeted clinical assessments and appropriate testing when required. This may include in-clinic point-of-care testing or external pathology services to better understand each patient’s unique physiology.

Treatments may include naturopathy, acupuncture, clinical nutrition, botanical medicine, lifestyle medicine, and other evidence-informed natural therapies, all applied within a modern scientific framework and guided by careful clinical observation.

Acupuncture - Regulating the Body’s
Natural Systems

Acupuncture is a time-honoured system of medicine that has been practiced for thousands of years and is increasingly supported by modern scientific research. By inserting very fine needles into specific points on the body, acupuncture helps regulate the nervous system, circulation, immune function, and the body’s natural healing processes.

From a traditional perspective, acupuncture works by restoring the balanced flow of Qi (vital energy) through pathways known as meridians. When this flow becomes disrupted due to stress, illness, injury, or lifestyle factors, symptoms and disease may arise. Acupuncture aims to restore harmony within the body’s internal systems, helping to relieve symptoms while supporting deeper physiological balance.

Modern research has helped explain many of acupuncture’s mechanisms of action. Studies show that acupuncture can influence neurotransmitters, inflammatory pathways, hormone regulation, and autonomic nervous system activity — helping the body move out of chronic “fight-or-flight” states and back toward repair and restoration.
